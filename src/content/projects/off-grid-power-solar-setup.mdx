## Why we upgraded

Our original 400W array and lead-acid bank got us through fair-weather boondocking, but anything cloudy or cold meant either burning the generator or hunting down an RV park. With more long-haul travel on the calendar — and more time at remote sites scouting **Kingdom Farm** land — we needed real off-grid runway.

## What we built

- **800W array** on the roof (4 × 200W panels, tilted 0° for travel)
- **600Ah LiFePO4** house bank (2 × 300Ah, self-heating)
- **60A MPPT** controller, **2000W** pure sine inverter
- **50A DC-DC** so the alternator helps on overcast travel days

## Step 1 — Roof layout

We dry-fit the panels with painter's tape first. Two long edges face the breeze; that matters more than people think.

## Step 2 — Battery swap

The old lead-acid bay needed an extra inch of clearance for the new bank. A 30-minute fix with a jigsaw, but worth measuring twice.

<Callout type="warning" title="Disconnect everything">
Shore, alternator, AND solar before you touch the bank. Lithium doesn't forgive tools dropped across terminals.
</Callout>

## Step 3 — Wiring + commissioning

The MPPT and inverter wiring is mostly straightforward. The DC-DC charger took the longest because we routed a clean run from the alternator instead of T-ing into the existing harness.

## 30-day results

| Metric | Before | After |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Average daily kWh harvested | 1.4 | 3.1 |
| Generator hours / week | 6 | 0 |
| Days off-grid before recharge needed | 2 | indefinite (May) |

## What we'd do differently

- Buy the self-heating batteries first instead of upgrading twice.
- Run the DC-DC charger wires *before* installing the new bank.
- Skip the bargain MPPT — ours died at week three.
