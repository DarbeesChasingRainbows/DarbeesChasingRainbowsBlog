## Overview

We were tired of guessing how full our gray tank was — and even more tired of finding out the hard way. This is a simple Arduino project that watches both tanks and beeps before they hit critical.

## Problem

The factory tank monitor in our RV is famously unreliable. Sensors gunk up, readings drift, and you end up tapping the side of the tank like it's 1972.

## Goal

A non-contact monitor that:

- Reads gray and fresh water levels reliably
- Beeps a warning at 80% gray (and 20% fresh)
- Costs less than a single bad dump-station mistake
- Doesn't need an app or a cloud account

## Build Steps

1. **Mount the sensors.** The capacitive sensors stick to the _outside_ of the tank. No drilling.
2. **Wire to the Nano.** Each sensor goes to a digital input with a pull-down resistor.
3. **Power from the RV's 12V house bus.** Use the buck converter to step down to 5V.
4. **Flash the firmware.** See the [GitHub repo](https://github.com/darbeeschasingrainbows/rv-tank-alert) for the sketch.
5. **Tune thresholds.** Fill the tank slowly and log raw values to find your trip points.

<Callout type="warning" title="RV electrical work">
  Always disconnect shore power and the house battery before tying into the 12V bus. RV wiring is
  forgiving until it isn't.
</Callout>

## What Went Wrong

The first capacitive sensor I tried was rated for "any tank up to 20mm wall." Our gray tank wall is 22mm. Readings were noisy until I switched sensors. Lesson: measure first.

## Final Result

The buzzer goes off about 90 minutes before we'd actually have a problem, which is exactly the lead time we need to find a dump station.

## Cost Breakdown

- Sensors (x2): $24
- Arduino Nano clone: $6
- Buck converter + buzzer + resistors: $8
- Enclosure: $9
- **Total: ~$47**

## What I'd Change

- Add a small OLED screen with current % readings.
- Log to an SD card so we can see fill rates over a week.
- Eventually, push to Home Assistant via ESP32.
