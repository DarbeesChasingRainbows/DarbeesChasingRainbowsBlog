## Three slots, every day

We stopped trying to schedule read-aloud and started anchoring it to meals:

- **Breakfast** — a chapter from whatever the family book is.
- **After lunch** — picture books with the youngest.
- **Bedtime** — long-running series; one parent reads, the other tags in.

It's not pretty. It survives.

## What earned a permanent shelf

Books we re-read often enough that they live in the rig year-round:

- _The Wingfeather Saga_ — long, faith-shaped, kid-approved.
- _Little Britches_ — Ralph Moody is the best storyteller no one told us about.
- _The Green Ember_ — a series that gets passed around.
- A children's Bible we trust — currently the _Jesus Storybook Bible_.

<Callout type="faith" title="Why this matters">
  Read-aloud is the cheapest, most durable habit in our homeschool. It costs nothing, it works in
  any weather, and the kids remember it years later.
</Callout>

## When a book doesn't take

We give it three chapters. If it dies, we shelve it — no guilt — and pull something we know works. Reading aloud should not be a chore the family endures.
