## The name came first

We used the phrase _Kingdom Farm_ for two years before we owned a single acre. People sometimes ask if that's getting ahead of ourselves. Honestly — it was on purpose.

Naming a thing you don't yet have is how you keep faith with the version of it you hope to build. The name held us steady when listings fell through and seasons stretched.

## What we mean by "kingdom"

Not a brand. Not a private kingdom. The Kingdom — Christ's. We mean a small, working place that gives more than it takes:

- Food that feeds people who didn't grow it.
- A door that opens to friends who need a quieter weekend.
- Work the kids learn alongside us, not separately from us.
- A long view that survives our own bad seasons.

<Callout type="faith" title="Long obedience in the same direction">
  Eugene Peterson's phrase. It describes most of what we want Kingdom Farm to be — slow, faithful,
  unspectacular work, kept up across decades.
</Callout>

## What the "farm" part has to be

Practical. We are not chasing aesthetics or a feed. The farm has to:

- Pay for its own taxes and inputs by year five.
- Produce something a real local market actually buys.
- Be small enough that two adults can run it on a tired week.
- Leave room for hospitality — guests, slow Sundays, a porch.

## Why we say it out loud

Naming the long-term thing keeps the short-term decisions in line. Every RV repair, every move-day, every parcel scout is downstream of a name we said out loud and meant.
